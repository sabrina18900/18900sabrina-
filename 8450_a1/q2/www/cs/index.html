Florence Nightingale was an english social reformer and statistician. She is also known as the founder of modern nursing. Florence was first recognized when she served as a manager and trainer during the Crimean War, where she cared for wounded soldiers. She became an icon of Victorian culture, and later on laid the foundation of professional nursing with the establishment of her nursing school at St. Thomas Hospital in London.
